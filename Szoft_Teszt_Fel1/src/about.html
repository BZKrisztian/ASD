The Genre of Souls-Likes

Souls-like games are a subgenre of action role-playing games (RPGs) inspired by the gameplay mechanics and design principles of the Souls series, particularly Dark Souls(and before that, Demon Souls), developed by FromSoftware.


So, what makes Souls-likes unique?

  • Challenging combat: Souls-like games are known for their challenging and methodical combat mechanics. Players must carefully time their attacks, blocks, dodges, and other actions to overcome tough enemies and bosses.

  • High difficulty: These games often feature high difficulty levels, punishing players for mistakes with significant penalties, such as losing currency or progress. Mastery of game mechanics and learning enemy patterns are key to success.

  • Exploration and discovery: Souls-like games typically feature interconnected, non-linear worlds that encourage exploration. Players uncover shortcuts, hidden areas, and lore through exploration, often rewarded with valuable items and equipment.

  • RPG elements: Players typically customize their character's abilities, stats, and equipment to suit their playstyle. Progression often involves leveling up, acquiring new skills, and upgrading weapons and armor.

  • Minimalistic storytelling: Souls-like games often employ minimalistic storytelling techniques, with cryptic lore scattered throughout the game world. Players piece together the narrative through item descriptions, environmental storytelling, and encounters with NPCs.

  • Multiplayer features: While primarily single-player experiences, Souls-like games often include multiplayer elements such as cooperative play, where players can join forces to tackle challenges together, or competitive PvP (player versus player) modes.

  • Atmospheric world design: Souls-like games are known for their atmospheric and immersive world design, featuring dark and mysterious environments, haunting soundtracks, and intricate level architecture.

  • Risk and reward: Players must weigh risks and rewards when making decisions, as progression often involves venturing into dangerous areas to obtain valuable loot or defeat powerful enemies.
